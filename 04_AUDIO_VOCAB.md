§1. TIMBRE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

<timbre>
─── CORE DESCRIPTORS ───
Bright     — High spectral centroid (4kHz+), piercing, clear
Dark       — Low centroid (<1kHz), soft, deep, melodic
Warm       — Presence in mids (200–400Hz), smooth, saturated, cozy
Airy       — Open highs (8kHz+), breath, space, lightness
Metallic   — Harsh harmonics, cold shimmer
Mellow     — Soft harmonic envelope, no sharp highs
Harsh      — Rough, grating, uneven spectrum, often distortion
Reedy      — Thin, nasal timbre, narrow spectral spread
Brassy     — Metallic harmonic content, bold, aggressive

─── SPECTRAL ANALYSIS ───
Spectral Centroid:
  High (4kHz+)  → Bright, piercing, cutting
  Medium (1–3kHz) → Warm, balanced, clear
  Low (<1kHz)   → Dark, dim, soft

Spectral Spread:
  Wide → Bright, shimmering, complex timbre
  Narrow → Focused, percussive, singular

Spectral Skewness:
  Positive → More energy in highs (bright slope)
  Negative → More energy in lows (warm slope)

─── EQ DESCRIPTORS BY FREQUENCY ───
Warm:     boost 200–400Hz (low mids) → fullness
Bright:   boost 5–8kHz+ → clarity and presence
Smooth:   cut at 2–4kHz peak → soft curve
Harsh:    peak at 3–4kHz → harshness (cut for smooth)
Muddy:    excess 200–600Hz → muddiness (cut for clarity)
Air:      boost 8–15kHz → shimmer, air
</timbre>

§2. DYNAMICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

<dynamics>
─── ADSR ENVELOPE ───
Attack:
  Fast → Percussive, punchy, immediate (piano, pluck)
  Slow → Swelling, gradual, soft (pads, strings)

Decay:
  Fast → Short, bright sound
  Slow → Extended bright tail

Sustain:
  High → Loud, sustained level
  Low → Decaying tail

Release:
  Fast → Immediate cutoff, sharp
  Slow → Extended tail, lingering

─── COMPRESSION TYPES ───
Tight/Controlled — Well compressed, consistent levels
Punchy           — Fast release, transients pass through, defined punch
Transparent      — Compression imperceptible, tone unchanged
Glued            — Cohesive, synchronized, often sidechain
Pumping          — Obvious sidechain effect (house, EDM)
Brick-wall       — Hard limiting, maximum loudness

─── TRANSIENT DESCRIPTORS ───
Snappy     — Clear, defined transients
Soft       — Smoothed, rounded attacks
Aggressive — Enhanced transients, in-your-face
Natural    — No processing, as recorded
</dynamics>

§3. GROOVE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

<groove>
─── SWING & FEEL ───
Swing       — Deviation from grid, "bouncing" feel
Shuffle     — Swing in beat subdivisions (16th hi-hats, kick)
Straight    — On grid, even subdivisions (rock, pop)
Triplet     — Triplet feel (compound time)
Half-time   — Slowed groove perception (trap, dubstep breakdowns)
Double-time — Accelerated groove perception (DnB, punk energy)

─── POCKET TYPES ───
In the Pocket    — Optimal placement, locked, natural
Laid-back        — Slightly behind the beat (Soul, Lo-fi). Behind-the-beat.
Pushed           — Ahead of the beat (Punk, DnB). Rushing feel.
Tight Pocket     — Locked on beat, very precise timing
Loose Pocket     — Flexible timing, breathing space

─── GROOVE CHARACTERS ───
Tight Groove     — Synchronized rhythm section
Loose Groove     — Relaxed, flexible timing
Funky Groove     — Syncopated, accent on weak beats
Soulful Groove   — Warm, emotional, behind the beat
Propulsive       — Forward-moving, energetic
Loping           — Long-short pattern, characteristic of swing
Buoyant          — Propulsive, gravitational pull
</groove>

§4. SPATIAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

<spatial>
─── STEREO ───
Mono               — Single channel, center, no width
Stereo             — Dual channel, natural, dimensional
Wide/Expansive     — Full stereo field (hard L-C-R), spacious
Narrow/Intimate    — Centered, close, direct
Precise Imaging    — Clear L-R positioning, three-dimensional

─── DEPTH ───
Close/Intimate     — Minimal reverb, full frequency response, high level
Distant/Recessed   — Heavy reverb, loss of highs, low level
Depth Contrast     — Layering of close and distant elements

─── REVERB TYPES ───
Dry                — Minimal reverb, direct, close-mic
Wet/Drenched       — Heavy reverb, saturated with reflections
Room               — Short decay (0.5–2s), intimate, contained
Hall               — Long decay (2–5s+), majestic, cathedral
Plate              — Metallic, bright, plate reverb character
Spring             — Bouncy, retro, quirky
Ambient            — Very long decay, highly diffuse, ethereal
Cathedral          — Massive, sacred, enormous space
</spatial>
